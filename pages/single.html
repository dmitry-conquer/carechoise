The decision to get personal care for seniors in Philadelphia is not one to take lightly. It’s a money investment and a big change for loved ones. Seniors must admit they need extra help- which is not easy.

However, if you notice certain changes in your loved one’s behavior, personal care may be the best thing. And while there may be initial resistance, families that choose home care often find it’s the best decision they’ve ever made. Here are some signs that indicate your family is ready to make that step.

What are Some Signs That My Loved One May Need Personal Care for Seniors in Philadelphia

Mobility Issues

Seniors often find it difficult to get around as they age. They may have conditions like arthritis, osteoporosis, diabetes, or injuries that reduce mobility. It may be difficult for them to complete daily tasks.

A caregiver can assist a senior with mobility issues by helping them with daily tasks. They can fetch items and help them transfer into chairs, baths, and beds. The home aide may also engage your loved one in physical activities that increase range of motion and mobility.

Difficulty Managing Daily Tasks

A loved one experiencing declining health may have difficulty managing daily tasks. They may be unable to clean, cook, and run errands. Seniors may fall behind and miss important deadlines.

A caretaker can handle daily tasks. They may also help seniors organize and make systems easier. For example, they may automate bill-paying or order home delivery to simplify their clients’ lives.

Declining Hygiene

Seniors’ limited mobility may mean they are unable to perform self-care tasks. They may be unable to wash, bathe, and groom. Loved ones may be embarrassed to admit these limitations, but you may notice them without being told.

Caretakers can help with self-care. They can assist seniors with bathing and toileting. They can help them dress, do laundry, and groom, helping them regain self-confidence.

Mental Health Issues

Seniors often deal with depression due to isolation and failing health. You may notice that your loved one seems down in the dumps. Symptoms include irritability, changes in appetite and sleep patterns, difficulty concentrating, and complaints.

A caretaker provides companionship, which can lift your loved one’s spirits. They can also teach them how to use technology so they can interact with others. Caretakers can take them to social events to boost their mood.
